Expressive art integration therapy in learning

(A therapeutic way of learning and teaching using Arts)

It is a expressive art therapy which integrate multi model approach that utilizes a variety of methods including various techniques of writing, music, visual arts, drama and dance to help learners achieve personal and academic growth. In this method pupils ( learner) work with a trained therapist educator, who helps them explore and understand their reactions to their experience with different forms of expressive art. It is a process of self discovery of knowledge and understanding that relies on artistic self- expression as a way to achieve set goals by creative processes using wide range of materials and techniques as a part of therapeutic intervention.

[ It is difficult to understand about pupil undergoing difficulties in learning by identifying the gaps. The use of expressive art will increase the chance of decoding the learning pattern and remedial activities at earlier age.]

Four main types of art are used in expressive arts therapy.

  • 1) Art therapy
  • 2) Dance therapy
  • 3) music therapy
  • 4) writting therapy
  • 5) behavioural therapy

Educator cum therapist who provide expressive arts therapy may draw on a wide variety of techniques in order to create teaching module that is best suited to the individual learner's need. Such techniques can include.

  • 1) painting or finger painting with acrylics or water colour
  • 2) clay sculpting
  • 3) Mask making
  • 4) dancing
  • 5) story writing
  • 6) poem writting
  • 7) psychodrama
  • 8) song writting
  • 9) role play
  • 10) improvisation
  • 11) sketching
  • 12) print making
  • 13) mosaic painting
  • 14) mosaic painting
  • 15) photography ( selfie creativity)
  • 16) mandala colouring
  • 17) cogngnative behavioural
  • 18) mindfulness art therapy approach ( MBAT)

How to start EAIIL
If you are trying expressive art integration therapy in learning you need not to be a psychologist or good in arts or painting etc. only you need to understand the techniques of doing it. The goal of this exercise is not to create but the process of creativity, which is matter the most. In class room focus in not on learning artistic techniques but on the inner emotional experience as learner engage in artistic process.
In class room scenario behavioural therapy is commonly used--------- it is an action based therapy and tend to be highly focused. The goal is to teach new behaviour or eliminate the issue. It can be done either by two ways

  • 1) classical conditioning
  • 2) operant conditioning

These techniques will help in identifying bipolar disorders, anxiety, attention-deficit/hyperactivity disorder, depression, eating disorder, panic disorders, phobias, obsessive- compulsive disorder Techniques used here are based upon short-term results with remidial activities.
We use cognitive behavioural therapy in class room for quick response and results within period of 15 to 20 classes.
